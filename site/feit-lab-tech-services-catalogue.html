Laboratory inductions and access

Delivery and coordination of laboratory inductions and access management.

What we do

  • Facilitate laboratory inductions at least fortnightly, with increased frequency during peak intake periods.
  • Publish and communicate induction schedules to lab heads and relevant stakeholders.
  • Support lab groups to ensure compliance requirements associated with local inductions are met and appropriately recorded.
  • Manage laboratory access permissions (core or afterhours) in line with training, compliance and regulatory requirements.

What you do

  • Provide user details early, including facilities requiring access.
  • Ensure all required compliance requirements (e.g. TrainMe and safety documents) are completed prior to requesting access.
  • Complete a Training Needs Analysis with new starters.
  • Inform with advance notice about visitors to the lab and their requirements: access, space, consumables etc.
  • Assist in identifying any new activities within your lab group that should be included in the lab induction.

Provision and maintenance of spill kits, safety showers, eye wash stations and first aid kits.

What we do

  • Coordinate weekly testing of safety showers and eyewash stations. (Compliance testing for these is undertaken by central University services.)
  • Regular check of first aid kits as per cyclic events. (Annual restocking of laboratory first aid kits organised by the HSW team.)
  • Provide a secondary check point for availability and validity of specialised first aid or emergency response items (e.g. calcium gluconate for labs using HF, PEG-300 for labs using phenol, EpiPen for food sensory trial areas).
  • Provide radiation badges where required.

What you do

  • Identify specialised first aid requirements relevant to your work.
  • Notify Laboratory Operations of missing or faulty equipment.
  • Alert the Research Lab Operations team of any issues with emergency equipment as soon as it is identified.

Provision and maintenance of general and specialised PPE; engineering controls for fume hoods and biosafety cabinets.

What we do

  • Arrange the purchase and supply of PPE and safety equipment for use in labs.
  • Ensure fume hoods are maintained by advertising service dates to users, providing clear instructions/tasks to complete beforehand, and checking units are cleared and decontaminated before the service date.
  • Coordinate annual service of all biosafety cabinets (BSCs), ensuring compliance stickers are up to date and service reports are filed for audit inspections.
  • Provide online and in-person training in the use of fume hoods and biosafety cabinets where applicable.

What you do

  • Ensure purchase and availability of PPE for the lab.
  • Ensure you and those you supervise have received and passed an induction prior to using a fume hood or biosafety cabinet, noting different models may require a new induction.
  • Clean these units after each use, including the sump area in biosafety cabinets, and ensure no items are stored long term within fume hoods and BSCs.
  • Comply with instructions/tasks to be completed before the service date.
  • Inform Lab Officers when a replacement or repair may be required.

Oversight and assistance reviewing risk assessments and safety protocols for research and teaching, and lab and workplace inspections.

What we do

  • Record required submission of safety documentation (SOPs, pRA, tRA etc).
  • Assist in development and review of safety documentation to ensure hazards are identified and appropriately controlled.
  • Provide direction on where safety documents are stored for each lab group.
  • Undertake workplace inspections in assigned communal and ancillary lab areas ad hoc or at least twice a year.
  • Support local lab inspections and audits undertaken by the HSW team.

What you do

  • Complete safety documentation to the highest standard, ensuring all details of the work, its risks and the emergency and disposal plans are completed and provided prior to work commencing.
  • As a supervisor, review the scientific information and ensure all safety precautions have been considered and captured in the safety documentation before signing off on the work being undertaken.
  • Complete regular workplace inspections in your areas of responsibility within required timeframes.
  • Support local lab inspections and audits undertaken by the HSW team.

Coordination of compliance obligations and audits.

What we do

  • Together with HSW, manage the audit process by attending all audits and actioning findings within required timeframes, including providing guidance and direction to lab users in pre-audit inspections to determine whether facility items require rectification or adjustment.
  • Oversee impacts on compliance guidelines in liaison with project managers and manage contractors to ensure they operate within the guidelines.
  • Provide advice and safety documentation for scheduled drugs, radioactive isotopes and chemical manifests.

What you do

  • Enrol new starters in the required compliance training in TrainMe.
  • Train new users in development of proper safety documentation and lab processes.
  • Encourage good laboratory practice in line with regulatory requirements.
  • Ensure any audit findings related to lab processes are actioned within the required timeframes.
  • Notify Lab Officers of: any unexpected contractor activities; any adverse events that will impact facility containment or compliance status; any planned changes to dealings or facility certification levels.

Support for correct handling and disposal of laboratory waste.

What we do

  • Provide guidance on waste streams and disposal pathways.
  • Ensure appropriate waste receptacles are available.
  • Coordinate contractor collections and waste transport as required.
  • Manage collection and transport of waste to the store.
  • Manage the collection, processing and disposal of biohazardous waste where lab technicians are engaged to support it.
  • Coordinate the laundering requirements for lab coats.

What you do

  • Identify and document the appropriate disposal path for all products before starting work.
  • Train students in correct waste disposal methods.
  • Ensure all waste containers are appropriately labelled, and constituents are clearly identified using University-approved labelling procedures.
  • Escalate uncertainties about the appropriate method of disposal for an item to the Laboratory Operations team.

Coordinate chemical purchasing, storage and facilitation of safe waste management and disposal practices.

What we do

  • Support lab users with practical and cost-effective chemical, dangerous goods and poisons orders by helping locate existing stocks and encouraging consideration of lower-risk alternatives to hazardous chemicals.
  • Coordinate chemical waste collection including manifest creation and ad hoc clearance of unwanted products, and ensure empty carboys and blank labels are available for chemical waste disposal.
  • Monitor storage management protocols for chemicals requiring specific management plans to prevent hazardous situations (e.g. crystallisation of picric acid).
  • Regularly review and clean out communal chemical storage areas and dangerous goods cabinets (annually), supporting lab users with any required actions.
  • Provide storage and disposal of scheduled drugs and radioactive isotopes.
  • Conduct annual audits and advise on the appropriate process, management and record keeping for scheduled poisons.
  • In conjunction with OHS, create and provide training, advice and guidance materials that support continuous learning on chemical storage and labelling.
  • Ensure appropriate storage facilities are available for products required for research.

What you do

  • Check availability of existing chemical stock before ordering.
  • Attach safety documentation and a requisition form to all chemical order requests.
  • Action regular reviews and clean-outs of lab chemical stores.
  • Train students in appropriate chemical and chemical waste storage and labelling (including aliquots).
  • Investigate and, where possible, substitute lower-risk alternatives.
  • Prepare and transport chemical waste to approved locations.

Support with orders, quotes and deliveries for faculty- and/or researcher-funded lab items including equipment, hazardous chemicals and consumables.

What we do

  • Liaise with suppliers for quotes and deliveries for faculty-funded lab items.
  • Purchase faculty-provided consumables through the appropriate University channels to ensure continuous supply, coordinating with import brokers where required.
  • Assist researchers with scoping new equipment purchases, including providing advice, confirming technical install requirements and checking scoping checklists are complete before purchase.

What you do

  • Ensure relevant Infrastructure professionals are consulted on all equipment purchases with sufficient lead time to ensure items can be accommodated and meet functionality, financial and safety requirements.
  • Submit required safety documentation with any purchase request.

Maintenance and replacement of core equipment in line with compliance, accreditation and manufacturer standards.

What we do

  • Fix lab facility issues as they arise (e.g. leaks, cracks, chips, power outages).
  • Coordinate with contractors to repair or install items affecting facility infrastructure.
  • Work with project managers to ensure projects are delivered as required.
  • Facilitate collaborations to ensure facilities are used at full capacity.
  • Raise potential facility upgrades or future demand items with School Operations Managers.

What you do

  • Report faults promptly.
  • Submit ServiceNow requests for small facility items (e.g. power circuit cut-outs, light globe replacement) and notify the team for follow-up or contractor coordination.
  • Discuss space requirements with Lab Officers to confirm available facilities or escalate requests if needed.
  • Notify of planned equipment purchases or disposals.

Regular monitoring and reporting of cleanliness and general upkeep of lab environments.

What we do

  • Provide feedback to lab heads, the HSW team and heads of department/school when the safety and compliance standards of lab users are not meeting minimum standards.
  • Meet regularly with School Operations Managers/coordinators and HSW to update on lab performance and current projects.
  • Report inadequate cleaning by contractors to University contractor managers and cleaning team supervisors.
  • Arrange annual cleaning and mould treatment of walk-in cold rooms.

What you do

  • Respond to concerns raised regarding safety or compliance standards in the labs you use.
  • Promote and encourage a culture of strong safety and compliance adherence in labs.
  • Report any issues with the central facilities cleaning service.

Management of communal equipment, servicing coordination and procedural oversight to ensure safe, compliant lab operations.

What we do

  • Within one week of a request, deliver training against the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) to lab delegates to enable them to train lab users on high-risk, authorised-user-only equipment (autoclaves; biosafety cabinets, laminar flows, fume hoods, microwaves; liquid nitrogen decanting; gas bottle management — transport, connection and disconnection).
  • Coordinate with relevant stakeholders (lab groups, contractors and Business Services) to schedule servicing of communal and safety equipment (communal equipment includes fridges, freezers etc; safety equipment includes pressure vessels, safety cabinets — fume hoods and BSCs — and safety showers).
  • Assist with managing contractors for non-communal equipment repairs submitted by lab users.
  • Manage a single reference repository of equipment SOPs.
  • Maintain functionality and expense records for communal and safety equipment, including supporting school budget planning by raising expenditure requests early for review, considering user feedback alongside records.
  • Maintain oversight across schools to identify lab equipment redundancies, dysfunction and duplications, including assessing condition, consulting with users and recommending repair, repurposing or decommissioning.
  • Coordinate access and usage requests for shared equipment.

What you do

  • Nominate lab delegates to be approved trainers for your group if desired.
  • Ensure delegates follow the approved training method for the given equipment.
  • Notify Lab Officers of any purchases or disposals you have actioned.
  • Ensure users complete required SOPs, pRAs and tRAs as required prior to using new items.
  • Provide feedback on equipment functionality and fit for purpose.

Accurate tracking of equipment and assets; fulfilment of asset lifecycle tracking, strategic planning and documentation requirements.

What we do

  • Update equipment registers on the designated asset management system, including details of new and disposed equipment provided through asset purchase request forms and asset disposal forms.
  • In collaboration with Faculty HSW, maintain a single reference document repository of equipment manuals, SOPs and Risk Assessments accessible to all areas of the faculty.
  • Confirm safety documentation availability for all new equipment brought on site.

What you do

  • Engage the infrastructure team prior to any purchases or disposals you have actioned.
  • Ensure lab users complete safety documentation for any new equipment prior to using it.
  • Store all equipment manuals, SOPs and Risk Assessments in the established single reference document repository.
